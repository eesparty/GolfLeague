5.0 Handicap

Returning player’s initial handicap will be 90% of the average of all scores from the previous year rounded to the nearest 1.  This handicap will be used until a new handicap is established after 3 weeks of play.

After a player has played 3 weeks a new handicap will be established using 90% of the average of the 3 scores rounded to the nearest 1.  All new players will use this handicap for weeks 1 thru 4.

After a player has played 4 weeks the handicap will be calculated as 90% of the average of the 4 scores rounded to the nearest 1.

After a player has played 5 weeks the handicap will be calculated using 90% of the average of 3 scores rounded to the nearest 1 after the best and worst score are dropped from the previous 5 scores.  Each following week’s handicaps will be established using the previous 5 weeks in this manner.

The maximum handicap for the League is 18

Makeup rounds will be used in the handicap calculation in the order of the week being made up

Example: If a player's scores are 41, 48, 45, 46, and 42 and par for each week is 36, the player’s
handicap would drop the 41 and 48 and use 45+46+42 / 3 = 44.33 – 36 (par) = 8.33 * 90%  = 7.4, rounded = 7.

6.0 Stats

6.1 Strokes and Putts

Each player will count the total strokes for each hole and each putt for a hole.  A putt from a surface other than the green (fringe, fairwary,etc.) is not counted as a putt.  With these records we can track Putts, Greens in Regulation, Eagles, Birdies, Par, etc.

6.1 Putting Out

Every hole must be putted out (No ‘gimmies’ shall be given).  If a hole is not putted in the player will receive the maximum double par for the hole.  This is done since we play everyone, not just our foursome and everyone is not there to give consent.

7.0 Scoring

7.1 General Scoring

After each weekly score is calculated using the player's handicap, each player will receive 2 points for every other player beaten and 1 point for every other player tied.  If a player is absent and the week is not made up, that player will receive the highest score of the week + 1 stroke and will receive 0 points.  If more than 1 player is absent, those players will receive points for a tie with each other.  The highest 6 point totals at the end of the year wins the prize money.  This type of scoring keeps everyone in the race even if a player misses a week. 

In the event of a tie in the top 6 spots the payout will be the total prize for the spots that are tied divided by the number of players tied. 

Example: If 2 players are tied for 2nd they are considered the 2nd and 3rd place winners will split the 2nd and 3rd place combined payouts.

7.2 Number of Weeks

15 Weeks will be used for the scoring (5 rounds per course).  The 16th week will be a non-scoring week and all prizes will be handed out

8.0 Low Net Score

A payout of $80 will be given for the lowest total Net score on each course (South, East, West).  The Net score will be calculated using total scores for all rounds played on the course minus that player’s handicap for each round.  All rounds must be played during regular league play so no makeup rounds will be used.  If no player has played all 5 rounds on a particular course the payout will go to the best Net score for 4 rounds and so on.

9.0 Golfer of the Week Payout             

Each week the player with the lowest score with handicap will receive $25 (based on 24 players).  The winner must be present for regular league play (makeup rounds are not eligible).  In the event of a Tie, the money will be split evenly.

10.0 Makeups ans Subs

- Makeups

  • Each player is financially responsible for their makeup round.  A witness (preferably another league member) must play and sign the score card before it is turned in.  The receipt is also required along with the scorecard
  • If a week is going to be missed a makeup can be played before hand as long as the round is declared a makeup for the specific date to be missed.  In the event of a cancelation for the missed week the makeup round will be null and void.
  • If a week is missed and a makeup round has not yet been played, the round can only be made up before the next week of play. 
  • There are no makeups for the final scoring week, players must play during scheduled league time to get credit for their round.
  • Out of order makeup rounds are not allowed.  If a week is missed and it's not made up before the following week of league play the player will not be allowed to make it up. 
  • All scores/receipts for makeup rounds must be reported before the following week of league play or the score will not be counted.

- Subs

If there is an opening because of an absence a sub can golf in the available slot.  The sub will pay the league $10 and have the option to rent a cart.  Sub scores do not count towards the league and subs are not required if there are weekly openings.  Subs are just a way to build the fund for the Golfer of the Week payout.

11.0 Closest to the Pin (Optional)

A weekly Closest to the Pin will be combined with a Year Closest to the Pin.  The entry fee is $60 which is $1 per hole weekly (2 holes/course) and $5 each for 6 holes in the Year game.  Each closest shot will win the weekly payout and be measured to the inch and logged by the last group.  If a week has no winner, the payout will carry over to the next time that hole is played.  If there are no winners the final week, the payout will be distributed equally among the players.  The weekly payouts for each hole will be $1 x No. Players.  The shortest distance to each hole at the end of the season will receive a year payout of $5 x No. Players.  The winner must be present for regular league play (makeup rounds are not eligible).  There will be no closest to the pin for the non-scoring week.

Note: All players will play the Yellow Tees on the East Course Hole #7 and the West Course #4

13.0 General Rules

  1. Have Fun!
  2. Every hole is to be putted out, no “gimmies”.  If a hole is not putted out that player will receive double par for the hole. 
  3. Any ball out of bounds should be played by Line of Flight
  4. "Rolling" the Ball is allowed to improve the lie but shouldn't change the lie or the line to the green, i.e. no moving from the woods to the rough, from rough to fairway or from behind obstacles
  5. The max score for each hole is double par
